## Tweet 1

Managing social media as a developer usually means dealing with bloated web UIs.

Why not manage your posts like code? 

I just built `postctl` — a terminal-native, Markdown-first scheduler and publisher for all major platforms! 🧵

## Tweet 2

With `postctl`, your drafts are just Markdown files.

- Write in your favorite editor
- Control target platforms & schedules in Frontmatter
- Version control your posts with Git
- Peer review your content via Pull Requests!

## Tweet 3

It comes with a fully interactive Terminal User Interface (TUI):
- Edit posts using an inline text area (or press `ctrl+v` to jump into Neovim)
- Open a terminal calendar datepicker (`ctrl+d`)
- Manage multi-platform accounts and check connection statuses in one dashboard

## Tweet 4

Fully integrated:
- Direct support for Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Threads, Mastodon, Bluesky, and Facebook.
- Background scheduler daemon (written in Go/SQLite).
- Stretched AES-256-GCM encryption to safely backup your database in public Git repositories.
