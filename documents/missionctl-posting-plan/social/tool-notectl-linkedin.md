📓 Introducing notectl: Local Markdown & Obsidian Note Management from the Terminal

Your notes represent your thoughts, ideas, and knowledge base. For developers and privacy advocates, keeping notes in proprietary cloud systems is a security and vendor lock-in risk. 

notectl lets you index, search, and edit your local Markdown vaults (like Obsidian or Logseq) directly from the command line.

Key Features:
1️⃣ Monospace Indexing: Indexes your vault's Markdown files into a local SQLite database, enabling blazing-fast full-text searches (`notectl search "query"`).
2️⃣ Daily Notes Automation: Automatically handles templates for daily logs, allowing you to open or append content to today's daily log with a single key.
3️⃣ Editor Integration: Press `ctrl+v` in the TUI to spawn Vim/Neovim, write your note, and sync it back on save.
4️⃣ MCP Server for Obsidian: Exposes 7 tools to Claude, allowing the AI to read notes, write new files, and append structured updates under specific headings in your vault.

Give your AI assistant access to a secure, local knowledge base without syncing anything to the cloud.
