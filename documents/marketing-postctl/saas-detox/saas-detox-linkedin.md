🔒 SaaS Detox: Why Local-First Social Media Scheduling Matters

Every modern SaaS tool wants to own your data, lock you into a subscription, and track your activity. But why should you pay a monthly fee and send your api keys to the cloud just to schedule text posts and images?

I built postctl to offer a privacy-first, local-first alternative for developers and creators.

Here is why local-first is the better path:
1️⃣ SQLite Power: All your tokens, schedules, and drafts are stored in a fast, self-contained SQLite file on your local machine.
2️⃣ End-to-End Privacy: No external servers intermediate your postings. The TUI background worker or local daemon speaks directly to platform APIs.
3️⃣ Zero Telemetry: Your typing, scheduling, and analytics remain strictly yours.
4️⃣ AES-256 Backups: Export your entire database into an encrypted binary file, ready to version control or sync privately.

Stop paying for bloated SaaS. Take back control of your developer workflow.
